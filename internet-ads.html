Internet-Based Advertising Policy

Internet-based advertising, also known as ad targeting or online behavioral advertising (“OBA”), involves tracking consumers’ online activities to deliver tailored or personalized advertising. This practice, while often invisible to users, enables businesses to align their advertisements with the inferred interests and preferences of their audiences.

Most Internet users who conduct searches for products or services have encountered behavioral advertising. In a typical case of OBA, your web-browsing activity is tracked through the use of “cookies” — small text files that a website’s server places on your browser. These cookies transmit information back to the website about your browsing behavior and may store data such as login details or items placed in a virtual shopping cart.

In certain contexts, such as advertising networks, cookies can be used to track users across multiple websites. For example, if you visit a travel website and then see travel-related ads on a news website, that’s an instance of cross-site behavioral advertising.

About Cookies and Tracking

Although it’s possible to opt out of certain types of tracking and delete cookies, some cookies (commonly referred to as “persistent” or “zombie” cookies) may be automatically recreated after deletion. To learn more about how we use cookies, please review our Cookies Policy.

Why We Use Targeted Advertising

We and our advertising partners may use information collected from cookies or similar technologies to serve you with ads that are more relevant to your interests. This helps improve your browsing experience and ensures that the advertisements you see are more closely related to your needs or preferences.

For example, if you have browsed antivirus or security software on our website, you may later see promotional ads related to antivirus products on other sites that you visit.

Your Choices and Control

You can control how your information is used for advertising purposes:

  • Adjust your browser settings to block or delete cookies.
  • Opt out of personalized ads via Google Ads Settings or similar ad network settings.
  • Use browser extensions or built-in tools to block tracking cookies.
  • Refer to our Privacy Policy for more information about how we use and share your data.

Transparency and Privacy Commitment

We understand that online behavioral advertising raises privacy concerns. At Nova Shield, we use these technologies responsibly and only in compliance with applicable data protection laws. Our intention is not to invade your privacy but to enhance your online experience with relevant content and offers.
